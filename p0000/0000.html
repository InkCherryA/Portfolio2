TORQUE HOUSE | 2019
This project is part of the studio course Architectonic Plasticity: experiments in points, lines, surfaces & volumes taught at the Cooper Union, by Mersiha Veledar, Benjamin Aranda, and Jonah Rowen. This course is an exploration of spatial verbs, geometries, and materiality.
This project attempts to visualize the formal qualities of the verb torque, which led to a series of geometric and material manipulations.
The project begins with a transformation between basic geometric elements: points, lines, surfaces. The animation demonstrates how a line going through a point is transformed into a sinusoidal curve, which is arrayed, at different states, around other points to populate a two-dimensional field. This field is then translated and extruded along the third dimension in a similarlly sinuoidal manner.
